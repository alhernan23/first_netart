VIDEO ART: the third of the saga

For our video art, we were to sync up our animation to our sound art. This was a challenge for me, not only because animation has always been something I struggled with but also because my sound art is just all over the place. It felt intimidating trying to brainstorm ideas.

After looking at some examples though, I had a better understanding that it was supposed to be an abtract animation. For things like this, you just have to not think about it and do whatever it feels like. I ended up coming up with the idea of this little square going on a journey to find a home (keeping it consistent with my friendship theme: my friends have always been my home).

I was mostly inspired by the “outernet” we saw in the film World of Tomorrow, I created about 62 frames in total, and had them repeated multiple times to get a minute worth of content. It was pretty difficult: cutting individual pieces of paper and taping it together, scanning each and every index card, getting enough frames and especially editing because everything I have is playing at about 0.1 seconds to make it work a little smoother (and also I was just following a YouTube tutorial for guidance on how to start). Working in Premiere Pro was really awkward because I haven't used the program before. I constantly copied and pasted individual frames, played around with the speed/duration a lot, played with effects, etc. But once I knew what I was doing (after watching a few YouTube tutorials), I kinda taught myself the rest and got into the flow of things. The effects make the process and the video itself a lot more fun, so that was one of my favorite parts of the animation process.
